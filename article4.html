But wait… What does "Direct Selling" even mean?

Direct selling allows distributors to circumvent the need for intermediaries in the supply chain by instead conducting business with end-users directly.On traditional sales, products can be purchased in conventional retail settings either online or in person at a physical store. On the other hand, direct selling is promoted by independent promoters (as you being a SAFIR Promoter) meeting with potential customers of their circles, in environments that are not conventional.

A known definition states:

“It is generally accepted that direct sales, also known as people-to-people sales, refer to the marketing of goods and services directly to the consumer outside of a commercial facility, employing a personalized presentation given by an independent promoter of the company. This final characteristic differentiates direct sales from so-called distance sales, which take place when there is no personal interaction between the company doing the selling and the person doing the purchasing.”

Another approach to the concept says that “single-level marketing” is one type of direct selling. In this type of marketing, a promoter earns money by purchasing products from a parent organization and then selling those products directly to customers. Multi-level marketing is another type of direct selling (in which the independent promoter may earn money from both direct sales to customers and by sponsoring new independent promoters and potentially earning a commission from their sales), and the human centered Multi-level Marketing is the one we encourage at SAFIR on our Promoters, known as Relational Marketing.

According to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC), "direct selling" is a catch-all term that encompasses a variety of business forms premised on people-to-people selling in locations other than a retail establishment, such as social media platforms or the home of the promoter or prospective customer. Internet sales (if there’s a previous contact with the person) are just one kind of modern direct selling, which also includes personal contact agreements such as one-on-one demos, party plan sales, and other personal contact arrangements.

Direct Selling is defined as "the direct personal presentation, demonstration, and sale of items and services to consumers, typically in the consumers' homes or at their places of employment," according to the definition provided by some sources.”

Important! A resumed definition would be: Direct selling is a way of selling with human interaction per se, and intentional from both parts. One to show, and the other to observe; with the product/service as the focus of the conversation.

It’s not luck, nor improvisation: the process behind Direct Selling

Direct selling removes the need for several intermediaries, including wholesalers and regional distribution centers, which are normally involved in the distribution of products.

Via a distributor or a promoter is the only way to purchase the products or services that are sold through direct sales because the products that are sold through direct sales are typically not found in traditional retail outlets.

In most cases, party-plan and network marketing businesses are thought of when referring to direct selling. Although these businesses make use of direct sales, they are not the only ones to do so. Many companies that sell business-to-business (B2B) make use of direct sales to target and sell to their end customers.

Unlike the other direct sales models, in MLM or multilevel there is a psychological component of support, reinforcement, guidance, and company for the team that one integrates.

! Important note! Direct marketing and direct selling are two entirely different things. Direct selling is when individual promoters directly reach out to consumers, whereas direct marketing is when a company markets directly to the consumer and is also known as business-to-consumer (B2C).

The sequence that experienced promoters use in Direct Selling is the following:

  • Contact: This is the initial point of contact between yourself and the prospective customer. The way, the quantity, the quality, the speech, and all the factors of contact are items you will collect via experience by practice, guidance, and study.
  • Presentation: Presentation is the most time-consuming step in the process, and its purpose is to demonstrate the qualities of the product or service as well as the advantages of using it.
  • Closing: The sale will be finalized at this point, which is referred to as the close.
  • Follow-up: It is essential to conduct follow-up, regardless of whether or not a sale was made.

What about Pyramid Schemes?

Because pyramid schemes and legitimate multi-level marketing company opportunities exhibit many of the same traits, it is unfortunately common to find it difficult to differentiate between the two.

Both multi-level marketing (MLM) and pyramid schemes need participants (who are referred to as "distributors") to recruit other individuals, and both MLM and pyramid schemes relate an individual's remuneration directly to their level of success in recruiting others. The primary distinction between the two is that pyramid schemes are intended to maintain a steady flow of money into the organization from the various distributors involved.

The pyramid scheme is also understood as when a company maintains the flow of money by charging fees and forcing distributors to buy products, even if they do not need or sell them, and are forced to accumulate them in order to reach a volume that allows them to get an economic compensation.
