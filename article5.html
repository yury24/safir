As an independent Promoter of SAFIR, the duty of knowing our products (with their advantages and qualities) and our brand is an essential part of the promotion work, plus the study and understanding of what one is promoting.

Since blockchain technology is a very important part of our product portfolio, from SAFIR we want to share the basic general notions that our Promoters should know before even inquiring about the blockchain products of our promotion network.

Therefore, the fundamental and first question that we must ask ourselves is:

What is blockchain and how does it work?

Blockchain, or “chain of blocks”, is a technology in its infancy (as opposed to the line of human technological progress), which could well be compared to a distributed digital ledger that stores data of any kind. What do we mean by this? In a chain of blocks, you can record information about cryptocurrency transactions, ownership of NFTs, automatic Smart contracts, deposits or deliveries, and DeFi voting, among many other possibilities.

If we compare it with the traditional recording and recording system in any industry or company today, instead of being kept in one location, by a centralized administrator (as it happens with an Excel sheet or a bank database), many Identical copies of a blockchain database are stored in several computers, called nodes, which are distributed in a network of computers that corroborate this data and participate in its annotation in an anonymous and totally public way. The “nodes” are computers that execute the registration action, in a public but anonymous system, where everyone participates in each data record, and no one is the full owner of the record itself.

The nomenclature suggests that the name of the technology, blockchain, is no accident: this “digital ledger” is often described as a “chain” made up of individual “blocks” of data interconnected in a single story. As new data is periodically added to the network, a new "block" is created and attached to the "chain". This process contemplates that all nodes must constantly update their version of the blockchain “ledger” so that it is identical in all “controllers”.

The way in which each of these new blocks is created is key to explaining why the Blockchain is considered a highly secure and "incorruptible" technology. Most nodes must verify and confirm the legitimacy of the new data before a new block can be added to the ledger. If we are talking about a cryptocurrency, this technology may involve making sure that new transactions in a block are not fraudulent or that the coins have not been spent more than once.

In addition, cryptocurrency transactions are protected by cryptography and must be processed by the nodes, which solve them through complex mathematical equations. What protocols like Bitcoin tend to do is reward the effort involved in validating this shared data with new cryptocurrencies.

It is worth mentioning that there are public and private blockchains. On a public blockchain, anyone can participate, which means they can read, write, or audit the data on the blockchain. Therefore, it is very difficult to alter the transactions recorded on a public blockchain, since no authority controls the nodes.

Private blockchains are controlled by an organization or group, which can decide who is invited into the system and have the authority to go back and tamper with the blockchain. This private blockchain process is more similar to an internal data storage system, except that it is distributed across multiple nodes to increase security.

What is the blockchain used for?

Blockchain technology is used for many different purposes, from providing financial services to managing voting systems.

Although blockchain could be used to store any type of information and to perform reliable verification of a person's identity, the most frequent uses of blockchain today are in the following areas:

  • Cryptocurrencies in general: The most common use of blockchain today is storing and recording data related to cryptocurrency transactions. The more people use cryptocurrencies, the more widespread the blockchain becomes.
  • Banking System: In addition to the widespread use of cryptocurrencies, blockchain is being used to process transactions in fiat currencies, such as dollars and euros, known in the industry as "fiat" currency. This could streamline these operations, since they can be verified more quickly and processed outside normal business hours without the need for or dependence on a person, also eliminating the margin of error that this entails.
  • Asset Transfers: Blockchain can also be used to record and transfer ownership of different assets. This is currently very popular with digital assets such as NFTs, a representation of digital art, and video ownership. However, blockchain could also be used to process ownership of real-life assets, such as deeds to real estate and vehicles. The blockchain would make it possible to verify the identity and ownership of a person in order to transfer the title deed without manually submitting documentation, which would be instantly updated on the blockchain.
  • Smart Contracts: Another blockchain innovation is self-executing contracts, commonly called "smart contracts." These digital contracts are automatically executed once the conditions are met.
  • Supply Chain Monitoring: Supply chains involve massive amounts of information, especially when goods go from one part of the world to another. Storing this information on the blockchain makes it easy to go back and monitor the supply chain.
  • Voting Systems: Experts are looking for ways to apply blockchain to prevent voting fraud. In theory, blockchain voting would allow people to submit votes that could not be tampered with and would eliminate the need for people to verify their identity or count votes.

Types of Blockchains

Given the characteristics of blockchain, it is easy to think which are the sectors in which this technology has the most room and future:

  • Banking: The banking sector was the first to incorporate the chain of blocks into its operational processes since this technology was born in a transaction environment. Blockchain can facilitate the commercialization of certain banking products and ensure the speed of operations, in addition to avoiding fraud risks.
  • Insurance: Blockchain allows more efficient management of payments and premium collections, in addition to reducing operational costs by streamlining all processes. As already mentioned, the blockchain is also very useful for detecting fraud and brings a lot of transparency to actions.
  • Telecommunications: The chain of blocks offers a series of advantages associated with authentication and security. Therefore, blockchain will have an impact on the telecommunications sector, especially in relation to network and business management. It helps to improve the competitiveness of the company and customer satisfaction.
  • Manufacturing: Blockchain allows different companies associated with the same logistics process to make safe and complete traceability of each stage of a product. In addition, the convenience of smart contracts improves productivity and optimizes logistics processes.
  • Medicine: Blockchain also makes it possible to easily follow the traceability of medicines. In addition, it allows immediate and complete access to medical records, treatments, and identifications by doctors, patients, and researchers. The chain of blocks favors the relationship between the patient and health personnel, which allows a more direct and personalized link.
  • Business Audits: Thanks to the qualities of the chain of blocks in terms of traceability and transparency, any organization can audit its activity without the need to interfere in the business processes in progress.
  • Media: Thanks to blockchain, communication professionals can reach audiences directly, without the need to go to intermediaries (media). On the other hand, content creators (not journalists) are gaining more and more relevance, and more money, with the creation of their information. For example, through the Steemit platform (based on blockchain), blog content creators receive payments in cryptocurrencies based on the popularity of their information.
  • Public administration: Several countries have already incorporated blockchain technology to generate a decentralized national administration and improve the experience of employees and inhabitants. For example, in Estonia, the blockchain is used to store tax information related to the national productive sector and the health of its citizens. In Australia, this technology is used in security, communications, and logistics activities.
  • Copyright: Blockchain gives authors the ability to have more control over the unauthorized use of their works. There are platforms (CoalaIP, Mediachain, Monegraph) that store the images and audio owned by users in databases that allow the automatic distribution of said works upon receiving an agreed payment. This technology allows authors to know when their works are used, thus being able to maintain the registration of their intellectual property.
  • Online sales: The chain of blocks allows the emergence of initiatives such as OpenBazaar, a platform that uses blockchain to connect buyers directly with sellers, without intermediaries. For example, banks can become tech companies with transactional software services, tech companies develop platforms with non-bank banking functions, and phone companies become a kind of media and identity management.

Main Advantages of Blockchain

Throughout the article, some of the advantages of blockchain have already been mentioned, but here are the most important ones:

  • Increased transaction accuracy: Because a blockchain transaction must be verified by several nodes, this can reduce the error. Plus, each asset is individually identified and tracked on the blockchain, so there's no chance of spending it twice.
  • No need for intermediaries: Using the blockchain, two parties to a transaction can confirm and complete something without the need for a third person or entity to mediate, saving time and money.
  • Additional security: In theory, a decentralized network, such as a blockchain, makes it almost impossible for someone to carry out fraudulent actions. To make forged transactions you would have to hack every node and change every ledger or manage more than 50% of the nodes. While this is not necessarily impossible, many cryptocurrency blockchain systems use proof-of-stake (PoS) or proof-of-work (PoW) transaction verification methods, which make it difficult to conduct fraudulent transactions.
  • More efficient transfers: These operations can be carried out from anywhere and at any time, due to the uninterrupted and unlimited operation of the blockchains.
